What is good design?

Design is a very subjective topic, but to me a good design is one that draws the eye, includes meaning that goes below surface level, and is cohesive. I have always enjoyed symmetry, I find that when something is symmetrical it tends to look more appealing and I find it to be more organized. I also find monochromatic designs to be very visually appealing. When something is made up of one color in different shades or is in black and white, I find that it draws my attention more and is more enjoyable. Not all designs I find "good" fit this description, but when I create my own projects they tend to fall into these categories.

I consider all of these images examples of good designs, but each image has a different reason for that distinction. Some I consider good because they are clear, sharp, and precise. Others I like because they draw my attention with their complexity and require more in-depth thought. Despite their many differences, each design is "good" in its own way.

In the Future

After graduating from Pitt I hope to go into the field of prosthetics. I want to do research in this area so that the approximately 35 million amputees around the world will have an improved quality of life. There is amputation performed approximately once every 30 seconds and those people deserve the best prosthetic possible to help them get as much normal function back as possible. The rapidly developing technology of today's world makes it possible to develop prosthetic devices that mimic the sensation of touch and have connections to the brain allowing the user to have cognitive control over the device. The possibilities in the field are endless and can drastically change the way millions of people live. This field connects my interest in medicine and my love of coding and design to congregate in one career. This class can help me succeed in the field of engineering because the ability to be creative, take criticism, revise, and present ideas in a group are all skills I will need on a daily basis. The knowledge I will gain about these skills as well as general design tools will be instrumental in my future career.
